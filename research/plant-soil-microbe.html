Phosphorus Phytoremediation

In my PhD research, I focused on mitigating nutrient pollution from historically fertilized ranchlands via the implementation of 'vegetation harvest strips' — a form of phytoremediation biotechnology designed to mitigate the loss of legacy phosphorus from soils.

This work was conducted primarily at Archbold Biological Station in Venus, Florida, in collaboration with Dr. Elizabeth Boughton and supported by the ForEverglades Research Enhancement Fellowship and the NSF Graduate Research Fellowship.
